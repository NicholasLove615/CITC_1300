What is Hockey

Hockey is a game, or sport, that's played between two teams, usually having six players that are wearing skates and compete on an ice rink. While there is other variations this is traditionally how it's played. The object is to propel a rubber disk known as the puck, past a goal line and into a net that is guarded by a goaltender or goalie. A fast paced and physical game that has become one of the most popular international sports. It is an Olympic sport, and worldwide there are more than a million registered players performing regularly in leagues.

Different Hockey Leagues

There are many different hockey leagues around the world. The most well known one, or at least where the best of the best strive to play in is the NHL or National Hockey League. Players from many different countries usually play to become a player in the NHL. The KHL or, Kontinental Hockey League, is an international professional hockey league founded in 2008 and is comprised of clubs based in Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, and China for a total of 22 clubs. It is considered the strongest professional hockey league in Europe. SHL, which is the Swedish Hockey League, is a professional league in Sweden and is the highest level of hockey in the Swedish ice hockey system. Founded in 1975 and consists of 14 teams. You also have the Liiga, otherwise known as the Finnish Elite League, that consists of 16 different teams and is the top professional ice hockey league in Finland. While there are many other leagues ranging from rookie to expert level in play, these are a few of the most well known leagues with higher level of competition.
